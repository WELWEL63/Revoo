Essential Travel Tips for Your Excursion

Your Ultimate Guide to Epic Egypt Excursions!

Start your day early to maximize time at popular sites. This helps you avoid crowds and experience the attractions in a more peaceful atmosphere.

Dress light and comfortable, but ensure your outfit is modest when visiting religious sites. Wear breathable fabrics and always bring a hat and sunglasses for sun protection.

Always carry a bottle of water, especially during desert trips or in the heat. Sunscreen is essential to prevent sunburn, and don’t forget lip balm for extra protection.

Respect the culture by dressing modestly, especially at religious sites. Always ask for permission before taking pictures, particularly of local people or sacred places.

Bring light clothing, a scarf, and sunglasses for shade. Wear comfortable shoes for walking, and pack snacks and water for long trips through desert or rural areas.

Cash is often preferred in local markets and for small purchases. Carry small change for tips and small vendors, as many do not accept cards.

Bargaining is a cultural norm in Egypt. Start with a lower offer, and don’t be afraid to negotiate in a friendly, respectful way.

Store important items like your passport, money, and valuables in a hotel safe. Consider using a secure money belt or a neck pouch while out on excursions.

Book through trusted agencies or ask for recommendations. Choose guides with good reviews and credentials to ensure an informative and safe experience.

Plan your itinerary so you’re not rushed, especially at larger sites. Take time to appreciate the details, and remember, some attractions require more time than others.

Get a local SIM card or ensure your phone plan covers international data. Having access to maps and translation tools is helpful during the trip.

Be mindful of your surroundings by not littering, and try to reduce waste. Respect the historical sites by not touching artifacts or graffiti.
